What it brings to your operations

From the map of what already burned to the map of what could burn.

Official systems tell you where there is fire and what weather index your province has. SilvIA Earth goes down to the level where decisions are made: the area, the hill, the plot.

Ignition risk prediction

The core of SilvIA Earth. We cross the state of vegetation fuel, topography and weather to rank your territory by ignition probability, updated throughout the campaign. It's the difference between deploying resources out of habit and assigning them on evidence.

Prioritization of preventive work

The risk map turns into an agenda: which firebreaks to clear first, which wildland-urban interface zones to inspect and where to reinforce patrols before the next heat wave. Prevention that is plannable and defensible.

Early warning of disturbances

When something changes on the ground (an incipient thermal hotspot, unauthorized logging, forest degradation…) the alert arrives georeferenced, with its severity and its confidence level. Your teams verify and decide; SilvIA provides the evidence.

Situational awareness in emergencies

During an active fire, a single view brings together what is today scattered across several systems: thermal evolution of the front, the state of surrounding vegetation and the wildland-urban interface, as support for coordinating the response.

Verification of restoration policies

Is the replanting from three years ago working? Vegetation evolution, measured from satellite over time, lets you justify with objective data the real result of every investment in forest restoration.

Integration with your systems

Risk layers and alerts can reach the tools your teams already use: your own GIS viewer or export to your service's standard formats.

Frequently asked questions

What we usually get asked.

How is it different from EFFIS or the official danger indices?

Official indices indicate the level of meteorological danger at provincial or regional scale. SilvIA works at operational scale: it ranks the specific areas of your territory by ignition probability, to decide where to act first. They are complementary, not mutually exclusive.

Does it replace our current alert systems?

No. SilvIA adds itself as a layer of anticipation and prioritization. Official protocols and your technicians' judgement remain in charge.

What does my service need to get started?

To define the areas of interest. SilvIA works with open satellite data: it requires no sensors or hardware installed in the field.

How does a public administration contract it?

Get in touch with the team and we'll study the route that best fits your service, from a scoped pilot to a public tender.
